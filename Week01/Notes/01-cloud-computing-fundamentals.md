
Cloud Computing Fundamentals

What is Cloud Computing?

Cloud computing is the on-demand delivery of computing resources such as servers, storage, databases, networking, and software over the internet using a pay-as-you-go pricing model. Instead of purchasing and maintaining physical infrastructure, organizations rent only the resources they need and scale them as demand changes.

⸻

Why Companies Use Cloud Computing

Traditional infrastructure requires organizations to:

* Purchase physical servers
* Maintain data centers
* Manage networking equipment
* Handle hardware failures
* Plan capacity years in advance

Cloud computing provides:

* Faster provisioning
* Lower upfront costs
* Global availability
* High scalability
* Automatic resource management
* Pay only for resources consumed

⸻

Essential Characteristics of Cloud Computing

1. On-Demand Self-Service

Users can provision computing resources without human interaction from the cloud provider.

Example:

* Launch an EC2 instance in minutes.

⸻

2. Broad Network Access

Cloud resources are accessible over the internet from laptops, mobile devices, APIs, and applications.

⸻

3. Resource Pooling

Cloud providers share physical infrastructure among multiple customers while keeping their resources logically isolated.

Benefits:

* Better hardware utilization
* Lower operational cost
* Improved efficiency

⸻

4. Rapid Elasticity

Resources can automatically increase or decrease based on workload demand.

Example:
An online shopping website automatically launches additional servers during Black Friday and removes them after traffic returns to normal.

⸻

5. Measured Service

Customers pay only for the resources they consume.

Examples:

* EC2 compute hours
* S3 storage used
* Lambda invocations
* Data transfer

⸻

Scalability vs. Elasticity

Scalability

The ability of a system to increase its capacity by adding resources.

Types:

* Vertical Scaling (Scale Up)
    * Add CPU or RAM to an existing server.
* Horizontal Scaling (Scale Out)
    * Add additional servers behind a load balancer.

Elasticity

The ability of a system to automatically scale resources up or down based on demand.

Example:
An e-commerce application automatically launches more EC2 instances during peak shopping hours and terminates them when traffic decreases.

Interview Questions

What is Cloud Computing?

Cloud computing is the on-demand delivery of computing resources over the internet with pay-as-you-go pricing.

⸻

Why is Cloud Computing usually cheaper than traditional infrastructure?

Because cloud providers share physical infrastructure across many customers, benefit from economies of scale, eliminate hardware maintenance costs, and allow customers to pay only for resources they actually use.

⸻

What is the difference between Scalability and Elasticity?

Scalability is the ability to increase system capacity by adding resources. Elasticity is the ability to automatically adjust resources up or down as workload changes.

⸻

Key Takeaways

* Customers pay only for what they use.
